Internship · Agni Steels

Production Bottleneck Analysis in Steel Manufacturing

Identified and analyzed a recurring delay at the cooler stage in a 5-step steel production line. Proposed structural fixes estimated to improve throughput by 12–15%.

12–15% throughput gain
Cooler root cause found
5-stage process mapped
Problem
Production delays observed at cooler stage — heat retention causing material buildup and downstream idle time at the ESP stage.
Analysis
Process mapped all 5 stages. Downtime logged per stage over 4 weeks. Cooler showed 2× average wait time (1.1 hrs/day) due to insufficient airflow and inadequate heat dissipation capacity.
Solution
Add secondary cooling system, improve airflow routing, schedule preventive maintenance at cooler stage before peak production hours.
Impact
Estimated 12–15% cycle time reduction; reduced downstream ESP idle time by ~45 min/day; improved overall production efficiency from 83% to target 90%.
Operations · MBA Project

Inventory Optimization Using EOQ Model

Applied the Economic Order Quantity model to a raw material procurement scenario. Balanced ordering cost vs holding cost to reduce working capital locked in inventory by 18%.

18% holding cost reduction
EOQ model applied
Live calculator built
Problem
Excess inventory holding cost eating into margins. Frequent stockouts also causing production stoppages — a classic over-order / under-order cycle.
Analysis
Collected annual demand data, ordering cost per purchase order, and holding cost per unit per year. Plotted total cost curve against varying order quantities to find the minimum.
Solution
EOQ formula applied: Q* = √(2DS/H). Optimal order quantity identified. Reorder point set at: ROP = Lead-time demand + Safety stock buffer.
Impact
18% estimated reduction in annual holding cost. Stockout frequency dropped to near-zero by setting proper reorder level and safety stock.
EEE Advantage · Energy

Motor Energy Efficiency Audit — Industrial Facility

Used EEE knowledge to audit 8 industrial motors. Identified overloaded and underloaded units. Proposed VFD installation to reduce energy consumption by ~20%.

~20% energy reduction
VFD recommended
14-month ROI
Problem
High energy bills in facility — suspected motor inefficiency. No prior audit had been conducted; motors were sized based on peak load assumptions.
Analysis
Measured load factor per motor using clamp meter and power analyzer. Found 3 motors running below 40% load (major inefficiency). 2 motors operating above rated capacity.
Solution
Install Variable Frequency Drives (VFDs) on partially loaded motors to match speed to demand. Replace the 2 overloaded motors with correctly sized units.
Impact
Projected 20% reduction in facility electricity consumption. VFD investment ROI estimated at 14 months based on current energy tariff rates.
Lean · Waste Elimination

Lean Manufacturing Waste Assessment — Shop Floor

Applied Lean 7-waste framework to a shop floor. Created a value stream map, identified top 3 wastes, and proposed targeted changes to cut lead time by 22%.

22% lead time cut
VSM created
30% WIP reduction
Problem
Long lead times despite sufficient capacity — non-value-adding activities were inflating cycle time without adding any customer value.
Analysis
Value stream map drawn for entire production flow. Top 3 wastes identified: Waiting (40% of delay), Overproduction (WIP buildup between stations), Defects (costly rework loop at final stage).
Solution
Introduce pull system (Kanban) to replace push scheduling. Set WIP cap between stations. Add inline quality inspection to catch defects early and eliminate the rework loop.
Impact
Lead time reduced by estimated 22%. WIP inventory dropped by 30%. Rework rate cut by eliminating late-stage defect detection.
