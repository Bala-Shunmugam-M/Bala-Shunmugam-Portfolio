Bottleneck Analysis — 5-Stage Steel Production Line
Process: Conveyor → Bunker → Kiln → Cooler → ESP
P → A → S → I
Downtime Data
Root Cause
Problem
Production delays observed at cooler stage — heat retention causing material buildup. Downstream ESP stage sat idle ~45 min/day waiting on cooler throughput. Daily output: 487 units vs 500-unit target. Manager attributed it vaguely to "machine age" — no data existed.
Analysis
Process mapped all 5 stages; logged downtime per stage over 4 weeks with stop-watch and operator interviews. Cooler averaged 1.1 hrs/day downtime — 2.4× the next-worst stage (Kiln at 0.5 hrs). Insufficient airflow and heat dissipation capacity confirmed as root causes via thermal inspection.
Solution
1. Add secondary cooling system to increase dissipation capacity. 2. Improve airflow routing to eliminate dead zones causing heat buildup. 3. Schedule preventive maintenance before peak production windows. 4. Install temperature sensors on cooler outlet to trigger early intervention.
Projected Impact
Estimated 12–15% cycle time reduction. Downstream ESP idle time reduced by ~45 min/day. Overall production efficiency improvement from 83% → 90% target. Formalised cooler maintenance interval prevents recurrence.

1
Insufficient heat dissipation capacity: The cooler's design cooling rate was adequate for the plant's original throughput target but not for the increased production volumes sought. Outlet temperature regularly exceeded safe limits, triggering automatic slowdowns.
2
Airflow dead zones: Thermal imaging identified two sections of the cooler where hot air was recirculating rather than being exhausted — effectively reducing active cooling area by ~20% and concentrating heat load on the remaining sections.
3
Reactive maintenance only: Cooler maintenance was triggered by breakdown, not by schedule. Each breakdown averaged 45–65 minutes of unplanned downtime vs ~20 minutes for a planned intervention. The formalised PM interval eliminates the breakdown premium.
4
ESP forced idle by upstream constraint: ESP downtime (0.1 hr/day) was not a machine problem — it was entirely caused by the cooler bottleneck starving the downstream stage. Fixing the cooler alone eliminates this cascading idle time without any ESP-side intervention.
5
Pareto confirmation: Top-1 stage (Cooler) accounts for 46% of total downtime. Top-2 stages (Cooler + Kiln) account for 67%. Classic 80/20 concentration — resource should be directed to cooler exclusively before tackling secondary stages.
